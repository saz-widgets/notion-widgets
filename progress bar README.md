# Reading Progress Widget for Notion
A simple embeddable progress bar you can drop into any Notion page to track your reading.

## How to use it

### Step 1 — Get your personal link

The base link to the widget looks like this:

```
https://yourusername.github.io/notion-widgets/reading-progress.html
```

Before you embed it, you need to add a little label to the end of the link so that **your copy of the widget is yours alone**. This label is called a `?book=` tag. It can be anything you want — the title of the book, a nickname, a number, whatever makes sense to you.

For example:

```
https://yourusername.github.io/notion-widgets/reading-progress.html?book=my-book
```

or

```
https://yourusername.github.io/notion-widgets/reading-progress.html?book=1
```

**The important thing is: give each book its own unique label.** If you use the same label twice, those two widgets will share the same numbers. If you never add a label at all, all your widgets will share the same numbers. Other than that, you really can't break anything — just pick something different for each book and you're good.



### Step 2 — Embed it in Notion

1. Open the Notion page where you want the widget
2. Type `/embed` and select the **Embed** block that appears
3. Paste your full link (with the `?book=` label at the end) and hit enter
4. The widget will appear — you can drag the bottom edge of the embed block to resize it



### Step 3 — Using the widget

- Click **"update"** to open the entry fields
- Type in your total pages and how many you've read
- The bar and percentage will update automatically
- You can also rename the labels (like "Total Pages") to anything you want by clicking directly on them
- Click **"hide update"** when you're done to collapse the fields and leave just the bar

Your numbers are saved automatically in your browser. They'll still be there the next time you open the page.



## A note on privacy

Your data never leaves your own browser. Nobody else can see your numbers, and you can't see anyone else's — even if two people use the exact same link. Everyone gets their own completely private copy.
